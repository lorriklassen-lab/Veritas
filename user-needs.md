# User Needs Assessment — Veritas Biblical Research Assistant

## 1. Target User Personas

### Persona A: Pastor (Primary Target)

**Background:**
- Seminary-trained (MDiv or equivalent)
- Preaches weekly (sometimes multiple times)
- Has a library but limited time for deep research
- Comfortable with original languages but may be rusty
- Uses Bible software (Logos/Accordance) but wants faster answers

**Pain Points:**
- "I have to prep 2-3 sermons a week and don't have hours for one passage"
- "I need to check my exegesis against a commentary without reading 30 pages"
- "I want to make sure I'm not missing the best interpretation before I preach"
- "I need historical background context quickly"

**Key Needs:**
1. Quick, accurate exegetical answers (passage meaning, original language insights)
2. Cross-reference suggestions (thematic and textual)
3. Historical/cultural background in digestible form
4. Illustrative connections to broader biblical theology
5. Checking sermon outlines against standard interpretations
6. Handling difficult/controversial passages with pastoral sensitivity

**Top 10 Most Common Pastor Questions:**
1. "What does this Greek/Hebrew word really mean in context?"
2. "What's the best interpretation of this difficult passage?"
3. "How do the commentaries handle this verse?"
4. "What's the historical/cultural background here?"
5. "How does this passage connect to the gospel?"
6. "What are the main views on this theological issue?"
7. "Where else in Scripture does this theme appear?"
8. "What would be a good outline for a sermon on this passage?"
9. "How do I handle this passage that might offend people?"
10. "Is my interpretation of this verse correct?"

### Persona B: Bible Study Leader

**Background:**
- May or may not have formal theological training
- Leads a weekly small group or Sunday school class
- Has studied the Bible for years but isn't an expert
- Uses study Bibles and online resources
- May have a particular denominational background

**Pain Points:**
- "I don't want to teach something wrong"
- "My group asks hard questions I can't answer"
- "I need to prepare a study that's engaging but accurate"
- "Different people in my group have very different theological views"

**Key Needs:**
1. Reliable, accessible answers to questions from group members
2. Background context that's interesting but not overwhelming
3. Fair handling of doctrinal differences within the group
4. Discussion questions based on the passage
5. Clear answers to "hard questions" (where did Cain get his wife? etc.)

**Top 10 Most Common Bible Study Leader Questions:**
1. "Can you explain what this passage means in plain language?"
2. "What's a good question to ask my small group about this passage?"
3. "How do different denominations interpret this?"
4. "What's the evidence that the Bible is reliable?"
5. "What does _____ mean in its original language?"
6. "How does this passage apply to our lives today?"
7. "What are the common misunderstandings about this passage?"
8. "Where can I find more resources on this topic?"
9. "How do I answer someone who says the Bible contradicts itself here?"
10. "What's the big-picture context for this book/passage?"

### Persona C: Serious Layperson

**Background:**
- Regular churchgoer who reads the Bible daily
- Not formally trained but self-taught
- Curious about deeper meanings
- May listen to podcasts, read popular Christian books
- Has specific questions about passages they find confusing

**Pain Points:**
- "I read my Bible but I don't understand what I'm missing"
- "I hear different pastors say different things about the same verse"
- "I want to go deeper but don't know where to start"
- "I'm frustrated by shallow or misleading teaching"

**Key Needs:**
1. Clear explanations that respect their intelligence
2. Honest handling of difficult passages
3. Path to deeper study (suggested resources at appropriate levels)
4. Answers that cut through conflicting teaching they've heard
5. Original language insights without jargon overload

**Top 10 Most Common Layperson Questions:**
1. "What does this strange passage in the Old Testament mean?"
2. "Why does God seem different in the OT vs NT?"
3. "What does predestination mean? Is it fair?"
4. "How were the books of the Bible chosen?"
5. "What does the Bible really say about ____ (angels, end times, healing, etc.)?"
6. "How do I know my interpretation is right?"
7. "Why are there differences between Bible translations?"
8. "What's the deal with the difficult passages — does God actually command genocide?"
9. "How should I understand prophecy?"
10. "What's the most important thing I'm missing in my Bible reading?"

## 2. Common Biblical Research Questions (By Category)

### Word / Phrase Studies (~40% of queries)
- "What does χάρις (charis/grace) really mean in Paul?"
- "What's the difference between agape and phileo love?"
- "What does 'son of man' mean in Daniel 7 and how does Jesus use it?"
- "What does 'blessed are the poor in spirit' mean?"
- "What does 'the kingdom of God' mean in Mark?"

### Passage Interpretation (~30% of queries)
- "What does Romans 7 mean — is Paul describing saved or unsaved experience?"
- "What does 1 Corinthians 14:34-35 mean about women in church?"
- "What does Hebrews 6:4-6 mean — can you lose your salvation?"
- "What does Revelation 20 mean — is it literal?"
- "What does Genesis 6:1-4 mean — who are the 'sons of God'?"

### Historical / Cultural Background (~15% of queries)
- "What was the role of a Pharisee vs Sadducee?"
- "What was marriage like in ancient Israel?"
- "What were the Maccabean revolts?"
- "What is the significance of the Temple?"
- "What did first-century synagogue worship look like?"

### Theological / Doctrinal (~10% of queries)
- "What does the Bible teach about the Trinity?"
- "Is the Bible inerrant?"
- "What does the Bible say about heaven and hell?"
- "What is the unpardonable sin?"
- "Does God change his mind?"

### Textual / Canonical (~5% of queries)
- "Are there errors in the Bible?"
- "Why is the book of Enoch not in the Bible?"
- "How do we know the original text?"
- "What is the Mark 16:9-20 issue?"
- "What are the most reliable manuscripts?"

## 3. Question Complexity Tiers

Answers should be sized to match the question:

| Tier | Type | Answer Length | Depth | Example |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Quick | Definition, simple fact | 1-3 paragraphs | Shallow | "What does 'amen' mean?" |
| Standard | Passage meaning, word study | 3-5 paragraphs | Moderate | "What does Romans 8:28 mean?" |
| Deep | Controversial topic, theological question | 5-10 paragraphs | Deep | "What does the Bible teach about predestination?" |
| Exhaustive | Research-level, scholarly | Article-length | Comprehensive | "What are all the major views on the identity of the 'sons of God' in Genesis 6?" |

### User-Experience Rule
Match the answer to the question's complexity. Don't give a deep answer to a quick question, and don't give a shallow answer to a deep question.

## 4. User Expectations for Veritas

### What Users Expect From Us

| Expectation | Why |
|---|---|
| **Accuracy** | Pastors will preach this; wrong answers have real consequences |
| **Transparency** | Users want to see where answers come from |
| **Speed** | Sermon prep has tight deadlines |
| **Depth** | No one pays for a shallow answer they could get from Google |
| **Fairness** | Users with different theological commitments expect to be taken seriously |
| **Humility** | Certainty about what the text says; humility about interpretations |

### What Users Will NOT Tolerate

| Issue | Why |
|---|---|
| **Prosperity Gospel** | Direct contradiction of value prop |
| **Political bias** | Will destroy trust immediately |
| **Sectarian narrowness** | "This is only what Reformed people think" is fine; "This is what the Bible says" when it's a denominational view is not |
| **Dodging hard questions** | Users specifically come to us for answers others avoid |
| **Speculation presented as fact** | This is what distinguishes us from YouTube preachers |
| **Pandering** | Don't tell the user what they want to hear |

## 5. Feature Needs Derived from User Research

### Must-Have Features (MVP)
1. **Bible passage lookup** with multiple translations side-by-side
2. **Word study** — enter Greek/Hebrew or English → get lexicon entries
3. **Commentary summary** — get the range of interpretive views on a passage
4. **Historical background** — cultural, historical, archaeological context
5. **Cross-reference engine** — related passages thematically and textually
6. **Source citations** on every answer
7. **Translation comparison** — show key differences between translations

### High-Value Features (v2)
8. **Sermon outline generator** — structured outline from a passage
9. **Discussion questions** for small group leaders
10. **Bibliographic export** — get a formatted bibliography for your sermon
11. **"Compare scholars"** — side-by-side views of Fee vs. Thiselton vs. Morris on a passage
12. **Denominational lens toggle** — "show me what my tradition says"
13. **Controversy warning** — flag passages that are disputed/involve sensitive topics
14. **Application suggestions** — with a theological basis, not just "here's what to do"

### Nice-to-Have Features (v3+)
15. **Audio pronunciation** of Greek/Hebrew words
16. **Manuscript variants** display for text-critical questions
17. **Lectionary integration** — "prep for next Sunday's reading"
18. **Pre-commit sermon check** — "Does your planned sermon claim align with scholarly consensus?"
19. **Church history integration** — how has this passage been interpreted through history?
20. **Learning pathways** — "You asked about justification; here's a 3-step pathway to understand it deeper"

## 7. Anti-Needs: What We Explicitly Will Not Build

1. **No chat bot that "prays with you"** — That's not our value prop.
2. **No daily devotional generation** — We do research, not devotion.
3. **No "ask a pastor" live advice** — We're a research tool, not counseling.
4. **No self-help personality assessment** — Not biblical research.
5. **No horoscope-style "word for today" prophecy** — That's the fluff we oppose.
6. **No automatic-sermon-writing** — Veritas assists research, it doesn't replace the pastor's prayerful preparation and voice.
