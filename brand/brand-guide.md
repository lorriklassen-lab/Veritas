# Veritas Brand Guide

> **Veritas** — Latin for "truth"  
> *"Truth from the Word — rooted, sourced, clear"*

---

## 1. Brand Essence

Veritas is an AI biblical research and study assistant for pastors, Bible study leaders, Christian educators, and serious laypeople. Our brand stands for:

- **Truthfulness** — Every claim backed by scripture and credible scholarship
- **Clarity** — Complex ideas made accessible without dumbing down
- **Humility** — Confidence in what the text says, humility about what we don't fully grasp
- **Rootedness** — Deeply connected to historic Christian tradition, original languages, and sound exegesis

**Personality:** Scholarly but not academic. Warm but not sentimental. Precise but not cold. Trustworthy like a well-worn study Bible, modern like a powerful research tool.

## 3. Typography

### Headings
- **Font:** EB Garamond (serif)
- **Weights:** Regular (400), SemiBold (600), Bold (700)
- **Usage:** H1-H4 headings, pull quotes, hero text
- **Reasoning:** Classical, scholarly feel reminiscent of printed Bibles and commentaries

### Body Text
- **Font:** Inter (sans-serif)
- **Weights:** Regular (400), Medium (500), SemiBold (600)
- **Usage:** Body copy, UI labels, navigation
- **Reasoning:** Modern, highly readable, pairs well with Garamond

### Monospace
- **Font:** JetBrains Mono
- **Weight:** Regular (400)
- **Usage:** Bible verse references, Strong's numbers, code blocks

### Type Scale (Reference)

## 4. Logo Usage Guidelines

### Primary Logo
- **Mark:** A stylized open book whose pages form a subtle cross shape at center, with a single gold line suggesting the "light of truth"
- **Wordmark:** "VERITAS" set in EB Garamond Bold, letterspaced +0.05em
- **Tagline:** "Truth from the Word" set below in Inter Regular, all lowercase
- **Clear space:** Minimum of one "V" character width on all sides
- **Minimum size:** 48px wide (digital), 0.75in (print)

### Logo Variations
1. **Full logo** — Mark + wordmark + tagline (preferred for hero/landing)
2. **Compact logo** — Mark + wordmark (for navigation, email signatures)
3. **Mark only** — Just the icon (favicon, app icon, social avatar)
4. **Inverse** — White version for dark backgrounds

### What NOT to do
- Do not recolor the logo (use only approved colors)
- Do not stretch, skew, or rotate
- Do not add drop shadows, outlines, or effects
- Do not place on busy backgrounds without proper contrast
- Do not rearrange the mark and wordmark

## 5. Design Principles

1. **Clarity over cleverness** — Every design choice should serve understanding, not decoration
2. **Earned trust** — The interface should feel reliable, grounded, and unhurried
3. **Minimal dignity** — Generous whitespace, restrained use of color, purposeful typography
4. **Scholarly warmth** — Professional but not cold; the feel of a well-loved study
5. **Accessible first** — Meet WCAG AA contrast ratios; don't sacrifice readability for aesthetics
6. **Consistency** — Repeating patterns build familiarity; users should know intuitively where to find information

## 6. Mood Board Description

**Visual direction:** A well-worn leather-bound Bible on an oak desk, morning light falling across open pages. Alongside it, a laptop screen with clean typography and subtle gold accents. Warm, natural materials — parchment, leather, wood. An atmosphere of quiet study and careful thought.

**Imagery style:**
- Warm, natural lighting (golden hour tones)
- Tactile textures: paper grain, leather, linen
- No people (focus on the text, the tools, the space)
- Color palette dominated by warm neutrals with pops of gold

**Keywords:** Rooted · Warm · Scholarly · Clear · Reverent · Modern-Classic
